THE JOURNAL

Pages from a worn notebook. Thoughts scribbled by torchlight. Prayers whispered under endless skies.

These pages trace His rhythm of rest and the hope of His soon return.

Day 5

Nullarbor Plain • KM 1,100

The Silence That Speaks

I turned off the radio somewhere past Balladonia. Not because I wanted silence—because I was afraid of it. But the silence came anyway. And in it, I heard something I'd been drowning out for years.

“Be still and know that I am God.”

— Psalm 46:10

WHAT THIS REVEALS ABOUT JESUS

Jesus withdrew to pray—He invites us to bring our noise to His quiet.

Prayer: Jesus, teach me to meet You in the silence.

Day 4

90 Mile Straight • KM 950

When the Road Runs Straight

146 kilometres without a single turn. At first it's boring. Then it's meditative. Then it becomes something else entirely—a lesson in trust. You can't see what's at the end. You just keep driving.

“Trust in the Lord with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding.”

— Proverbs 3:5

WHAT THIS REVEALS ABOUT JESUS

Jesus is trustworthy when we can’t see the end of the road.

Prayer: Jesus, help me trust Your lead when I can’t see the turn ahead.

Day 7

Bunda Cliffs Campsite • KM 1,450

The Weight of Stars

Lying on my back at 2am, I tried to count them. Gave up after a thousand. How small I am. How vast He is. And somehow, He knows my name.

“He determines the number of the stars and calls them each by name.”

— Psalm 147:4

WHAT THIS REVEALS ABOUT JESUS

Jesus, the Creator-King, knows us by name and holds the galaxies.

Prayer: Jesus, thank You for knowing me fully and loving me completely.
